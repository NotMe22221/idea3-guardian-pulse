01 What is it

When someone is being scammed — on the phone, in person, or even digitally — their body responds before their conscious mind catches up. Heart rate elevates. Electrodermal activity spikes. Voice stress changes. These signals are real, measurable, and AI-readable in real time. Guardian Pulse combines biosensors in a wearable with ambient audio analysis to create a continuous, real-time fraud detection system that protects people at the neurological level — not just the transactional one.

💓
Heart Rate Variability
EDA + PPG SENSOR
Sudden psychological stress — manufactured urgency, fear, panic — causes measurable HRV changes within seconds. The wearable establishes your baseline and detects deviations in real time.
⚡
Electrodermal Activity
GALVANIC SKIN RESPONSE
Skin conductance spikes when the nervous system activates — exactly what happens under scammer-manufactured urgency. EDA is one of the most reliable physiological markers of acute stress.
🎙️
Ambient Voice Analysis
ON-DEVICE AUDIO AI
Processed entirely on device. Analyzes the conversation for urgency language, isolation tactics, authority impersonation, and financial pressure patterns — in any language, in real time.

The key innovation: It's the combination that's new. Physiological stress + ambient conversation analysis together create a signal that cannot be faked by the scammer and cannot be missed by the victim. The body doesn't lie. The words don't lie. The AI reads both simultaneously.

03 Why this is 10x

Every existing solution monitors what you do — your transactions, your clicks, your calls. Guardian Pulse monitors what you feel. It's the first security system that operates at the human layer — the neurological layer — rather than the digital layer.

1

Works in person

The only existing solution that catches in-person scams — the courier at the door collecting cash, the in-person "government agent," the in-store crypto kiosk moment. No digital signal needed.

05 Why it can't be built today — but can be in 5 years
⚡

EDA sensors aren't small enough yet

Medical-grade EDA requires bulkier hardware. By 2028–2030: miniaturized biometric arrays in consumer wearables are on Samsung, Apple, and Garmin roadmaps.

🧠

On-device audio AI still too slow

Real-time voice analysis + biosignal fusion requires more compute than today's wearable chips can run. By 2027: Apple Neural Engine W-series and Qualcomm's wearable AI chips close this gap.

📊

Training data doesn't exist

No dataset pairing real physiological scam responses with verified fraud events. Buildable via ethical simulation studies, elder care partnerships, and opt-in data collection programs.

🔋

Continuous sensing drains battery

Ambient audio + EDA + HRV simultaneously running would kill a watch in hours. Next-gen neuromorphic chips cut this power requirement by 90% — arriving in wearables by 2027.
